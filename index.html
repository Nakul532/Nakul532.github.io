Published Work

📖

Probability of Heart Disease Using Various Machine Learning Algorithms

Nakul Shriman, S. Pillai, S. Mogra, K. Bakade · NMIMS · Published in peer-reviewed journal

Developed a comparative study using the Framingham Heart Study dataset (4,241 patients, 16 variables) to predict 10-year coronary heart disease risk. Pioneered Boruta feature selection and SMOTE class balancing. Random Forest achieved 83.96% accuracy, outperforming Logistic Regression (66.69%), SVM, Naive Bayes, and KNN across accuracy, F1 score, and AUC metrics. Identified age and systolic blood pressure as the strongest predictive features.
